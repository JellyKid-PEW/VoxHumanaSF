She crouched beside an open panel beneath the bridge console, sleeves pushed past her elbows, wire casing peeled back with the confidence of someone who didn't need permission anymore. She'd routed power twice. First out of necessity. Then out of spite.

The Wild Huntress didn't hum, exactly. She had rhythm. Low. Off-kilter. Too syncopated for factory specs.

Hold Two · aft of the galley

Most systems keep records. The Huntress keeps memory.

One crate in Hold Two, sealed, blue. Flagged, not logged for trade. That's the whole system in one object.

How it arrived is Signal — what the ship hears and what it answers. Why it stays is Hold — provenance, placement, attachment. What it comes to mean is Drift — the patterns that only show when you stop staring at any one thing. Those rooms open as the ship finishes pressurizing them.

    Strata — in preparation

    Some readers keep notes in the margins of exact passages. Strata will let you mark a line, keep a thought beside it, and come back to both — private by default, yours unless you choose otherwise. Later: Reader Lenses, collections of annotations shared deliberately, with people you pick. A Reader Lens is yours; a Perspective is the ship's. None of it required. The chapters are complete without it.

    Margins — the author lens

    Aboard the Huntress the important instructions aren't in the manual — they're on a card, taped where they're useful, in handwriting. Margins is that, for the book: my notes beside the text. Craft commentary, motif trails, audio fragments, reread material — spoiler-controlled, so it never runs ahead of where you are. It isn't a character's attention, and it isn't your annotations. It sits alongside both.
